## 2. Information We Collect

Depending on how you use Sushi Party, the app may collect or store the following categories of information:

### Account and profile information
- Display name
- Username
- Email address

### App content you create
- Session history
- Sushi counts and item-level logs
- Restaurant selections
- Session notes
- Tagged attendees and friend connections
- Quick-start templates
- Backup data you choose to export or restore

### Location information
- Approximate or precise location, only if you grant permission
- Location is used to help show nearby restaurants and to attach restaurant context to a session

### Device and app information
- Locally generated device/profile identifier
- Basic app state needed to support local storage and group session flows

## 3. How We Use Information

We use information to:
- Create and manage your local profile
- Save and display your session history
- Show friends, activity, and tagged attendees
- Help you find or create restaurants
- Support group session features
- Generate analytics inside the app, such as favorite items or restaurant insights
- Let you export, back up, or restore your own data
- Maintain, improve, and troubleshoot the app

## 4. Local-First Storage

Sushi Party is currently designed as a local-first app.

Most user data is stored on the device using local app storage, including:
- Profile information
- Session history
- Friends data used by the current app build
- Templates
- Backup/restore payloads that you choose to generate or import

This means your data may remain on your device unless you explicitly export, share, back up, or otherwise transmit it.

## 5. Location Permissions

Sushi Party may request access to your device location so it can:
- Show nearby restaurants
- Pre-fill restaurant context for a session
- Associate a session with location-based restaurant data

You can deny or revoke location permission at any time in your device settings. If you do, some location-based features may not work correctly.

## 6. Sharing and Data Exports

If you use export, share, or backup features, the app may generate content that includes your session data. That content is only shared when you choose to do so.

Examples include:
- Plain-text exports
- CSV exports
- JSON backup files
- Shared summaries through system share dialogs

Once you export or share data outside the app, that data is subject to the privacy practices of the destination you choose, such as email, cloud storage, messaging apps, or notes apps.

## 7. Third Parties

Based on the current app implementation in this repository:
- We do not include advertising SDKs
- We do not use third-party analytics SDKs in the shipped app code by default
- We do not sell personal information

The codebase includes Cloudflare Worker, D1, and Durable Object backend modules. Before production use, update this Privacy Policy and App Store privacy disclosures to describe any cloud-synced account, restaurant, session, group session, or profile data you actually enable.

## 8. Data Retention

Because Sushi Party stores most data locally, retention is generally controlled by:
- How long you keep the app installed
- Whether you delete sessions, backups, or app data
- Whether you use the in-app reset or restore tools

If you uninstall the app or clear app storage, locally stored data may be removed from the device.

## 9. Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect information handled by the app. However, no method of storage or transmission is completely secure.

You are responsible for protecting:
- Your device
- Any exported files
- Any shared backups or session exports
